====== Post-unblinding fit and inference results ====== This page shows the large amount of results and check-plots on the statistics analysis, with comparisons between Xephyr and blueice results. * [[https://xe1t-wiki.lngs.infn.it/doku.php?id=xenon:xenon1t:analysis:subgroup:wimphysics:sr1_statistics:sr1_statistics_validation:unblind_validation&rev=1526290470 | Results before desalination]] * [[https://xe1t-wiki.lngs.infn.it/doku.php?id=xenon:xenon1t:analysis:subgroup:wimphysics:sr1_statistics:sr1_statistics_validation:unblind_validation&rev=1524818392 | Result at unblinding: SR1 only analysis]] * 150508 Update to run combination results, AprilV2 inputs. * 150509 More masses * 180515 desalinated results ===== Shorter summary ===== The unblinded (and as of the 14th of May unsalted) SR1 data shows a small excess with respect to the median expectation corresponding to a 1-sigma upwards fluctuation. For both analysis codes, the best-fit signal rate is higher than 0, with a higher rate seen with blueice (which includes some higher radius events). Wall-fits are in line with expected numbers, and fits look to be good. Limits are a bit higher than the +1 sigma line as a consequence of the excess, both the best-fit itself and a wider-than-average likelihood. ===== Main results ===== The p-value is our main deliverable in the case of a large excess. It is defined as the probability, given the background only hypothesis, that the best-fit is equally or more incompatible with the background-only hypothesis. The p-value is uniformly distributed between 0 and 1 if the background-only model is correct. In our combined analysis, the p-value is just on the far side of the 1-sigma line, or indeed close to the 0-sigma line taking the global p-value into account. ==== Log-likelihood-profile ==== To investigate whether the likelihood minimization has converged to a true minimum, rather than a false one, and in general validate the profiling machinery, the below plots show the likelihood as function of all likelihood parameters. No clear problems are seen. One false minimum for the safeguard is seen, but the minimum achieved looks to be the best. Note that these are not profile likelihoods-- for each plot, only the parameter on the x-axis is varied, while the others are kept at the best-fit value. For the purpose of diagnosing fits, this is adequate, and much faster than performing a profile in each nuisance parameter direction. Lastly, the flatness of the AC likelihood is a reflection of the flat constraint put in. Note that a slight tilt drives the value to one or the other edge of the allowed range. Note about XEPHYR: the likelihood is build by divinding the whole TPC volume into 3-subvolumes, the full likelihood is then a combination of those sub-likelihoods, therefore the parameters that are
